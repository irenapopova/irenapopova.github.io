This page outlines the principles, themes, and working directions that inform my research and academic writing.

## Research Focus

My work connects applied linguistics, multilingual education, digital learning, and emerging technologies. I am especially interested in the relationship between language, cognition, structured learning, and computational thinking.

## Core Themes

* Artificial intelligence in education
* Natural language processing and language learning
* Applied linguistics and grammar awareness
* Multilingualism and adult education
* Computational thinking and programming education
* Blockchain and emerging technologies

## Research Approach

My research is grounded in analytical clarity, interdisciplinary inquiry, and methodological relevance. I examine the interaction between language, cognition, technology, and learning, with particular attention to the ways in which linguistic structures, meaning-making processes, and multilingual experience shape engagement with digital and computational systems.

I seek to connect theoretical perspectives from applied linguistics, natural language processing, education, and human–computer interaction with practical contexts of learning and technology development. This approach allows research questions to be considered not only at the conceptual level, but also in relation to their pedagogical, technological, and social implications.

## Ongoing Research

My ongoing research explores the relationship between language, cognition, artificial intelligence, computational thinking, and digital education. Current work focuses on multilingual learning, language-aware technologies, programming education, human-centred AI, and the development of practical research prototypes.

These areas will be further developed through academic publications, working papers, empirical studies, and interdisciplinary projects.
