Academic Profile

Interdisciplinary Research Profile

Independent researcher with an interdisciplinary background in applied linguistics, multilingual education, software engineering, and digital learning. My work examines how language, cognition, data, and computational systems shape the construction, organisation, and communication of knowledge in increasingly technology-mediated environments.

My research interests include natural language processing, human-centred and explainable artificial intelligence, knowledge representation, data integration, computational thinking, and educational technology. I am particularly interested in the development of multilingual intelligent systems that remain interpretable, context-sensitive, and responsive to human learning and decision-making.

Drawing on experience in linguistic analysis, academic writing, programming, and education, I connect theoretical perspectives from applied linguistics and cognitive learning with practical methods from software development and data-oriented research. This interdisciplinary approach supports the design and critical evaluation of digital systems that combine technical capability with semantic clarity, methodological transparency, and social responsibility.

My Workflow

My work connects language, cognition, digital education, knowledge representation, and intelligent systems design. I am especially interested in how multilingual and explainable AI can support interpretation, learning, and responsible decision-making.
